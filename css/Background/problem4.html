Pink Tree

Amidst the blush of dawn's embrace,
A bird alights on a pink tree's grace.
Its feathers, a symphony of rose and gold,
Whisper secrets to the morning cold.

Perched on boughs that blush like cheeks,
It sings a melody only the wind seeks.
Petals sway, enchanted by its tune,
As the sun ascends, painting skies in bloom.

In this quiet communion of earth and wing,
The bird finds solace, and the tree, its spring.
Together, they weave a delicate refrain,
A moment suspended, where beauty remains.
